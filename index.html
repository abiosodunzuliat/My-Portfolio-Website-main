Career Statment

Becoming a Disability Support Worker is more than a career choice for me. It is a calling that aligns deeply with my personal values of compassion, respect, and empowerment. I chose this path because I believe that everyone, regardless of their physical or cognitive challenges, has the right to live a life of dignity, choice, and fulfillment. This role gives me the opportunity to actively contribute to making that belief a reality for others. To me, being a Disability Support Worker means being a trusted ally in the lives of those I support. It means helping individuals feel seen, heard, and valued for who they are. My approach is to treat every person I work with as an individual, recognizing their unique abilities, desires, and challenges. I aim to offer support in a way that promotes independence, fosters self-confidence, and encourages a sense of control over their own lives. I want my clients to feel empowered to pursue their goals and dreams, knowing they have someone by their side who truly cares about their well-being. Ultimately, the career I aspire to is one where I can leave a lasting impact on the lives of others, helping to create a more inclusive society where everyone, regardless of ability, has the chance to live their best life. I want to be the kind of support worker who not only assists but also uplifts, offering hope, encouragement, and unwavering support at every step of the journey.

Career Growth

University Certification
Holding a BSc in Sociology has helped me understand the challenges people with disabilities experience, including barriers to inclusion and accessibility. It has strengthened my ability to communicate with empathy and cultural sensitivity. Additionally, my knowledge of social policies and support systems allows me to guide clients in accessing the resources and care they need to lead more inclusive and fulfilling lives.

CPR & First Aid Certification

Having a CPR and First Aid certificate is essential for me as a Disability Support Worker, as it ensures I'm ready to handle medical emergencies and provide immediate help if needed. This certification reflects my commitment to keeping a safe environment and being there for my clients in critical moments. It’s an important part of building trust and providing the best care possible.

Nafdac Certificate of Honor and Food Safety Certification

Having a Certificate of Honour from NAFDAC (National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control) has given me a solid understanding of health and safety regulations, including the proper handling and administration of medications and health-related products. This knowledge is crucial for ensuring the safety and well-being of clients with disabilities. Additionally, to broaden my expertise, I obtained a Food Safety Certification from Canada, as I wanted to familiarize myself with the safety guidelines and regulations specific to Canada. With this combination of certifications, I’m well-equipped to provide safe, effective care while adhering to both Nigerian and Canadian standards, ensuring the highest level of support for my clients.

WMHIS Certification

Obtaining a WHMIS (Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System) certification has enhanced my ability to maintain a safe environment for clients with disabilities. This certification has taught me how to safely handle hazardous materials, properly store chemicals, and understand safety data sheets, all of which are essential in preventing accidents or health risks. With this knowledge, I can ensure that my clients are protected from potential hazards while providing the highest level of care. The WHMIS certification supports my commitment to creating a safe and well-regulated environment for both clients and staff.
